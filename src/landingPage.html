Publications

Early neutrophil responses are potential biomarkers to predict severe COVID-19 in adults

In this article, we present compelling evidence that neutrophil mediators represent a promising dual-purpose target: both as a potential therapeutic intervention and as early prognostic biomarkers for severe respiratory viral infections. Our findings highlight the critical role of neutrophil-derived factors in disease pathogenesis and progression, offering a novel approach to understanding and managing viral respiratory diseases.

Singh, A., et al. Early neutrophil responses as potential biomarkers to predict severe COVID-19 in adults. J Leukoc Biol. 2025; 116(5):1184–1197. doi: 10.1093/jleuko/qiaf035

Age- and vaccination status-dependent isolation guidelines based on simulation of SARS-CoV-2 Delta cases in Singapore

Isolating infected individuals is crucial for managing pandemics, but extended isolation can be challenging for individuals. To ease this burden, we investigated a flexible isolation period strategy—adjusting the duration based on factors like age and vaccination status. Our results indicate that personalizing isolation can shorten the average duration by 1.4 to 1.8 days per person while maintaining effective infection control. This method reduces excessive isolation and still curbs transmission. Refining isolation practices is essential to strike a balance between safeguarding public health and supporting individual well-being. Explore our study to learn more!

Singh, A., co-author. Age- and vaccination status-dependent isolation guidelines based on simulation of SARS-CoV-2 Delta cases in Singapore. Communications Medicine, 2025. https://doi.org/10.1038/s43856-025-00797-8

Defining the Critical Requisites for Accurate Simulation of SARS-CoV-2 Viral Dynamics: Patient Characteristics and Data Collection Protocol

Understanding how viral load changes during infection is key to guiding public health responses. In our study of SARS-CoV-2 patients in Singapore, we found that older unvaccinated individuals shed the virus longer, while older, symptomatic, and vaccinated patients reached higher peak viral loads and were diagnosed earlier. We also identified optimal sampling strategies: a 21-day follow-up with testing every 3 days for Delta, and an 8-day daily testing protocol for Omicron. These findings emphasize the importance of tailoring both clinical expectations and data collection to patient characteristics and variant type.

Singh, A., et al. Viral load kinetics and patient characteristics shape isolation guidelines for SARS-CoV-2 Delta and Omicron variants. Journal of Medical Virology, 2025. https://doi.org/10.1002/jmv.70174
